Does Creatine Cause Hair Loss? What the Research Says

Creatine is one of the most popular sports supplements in the world, widely used to improve strength and exercise performance. But in recent years, concerns have circulated online that creatine supplementation may accelerate hair loss — particularly in people who are genetically predisposed to male-pattern baldness.

The concern stems primarily from a single 2009 study involving college-aged rugby players. Researchers found that after three weeks of creatine loading, participants showed a significant increase in dihydrotestosterone (DHT) — a hormone known to shrink hair follicles in individuals with androgenetic alopecia.

The DHT increase cited here came from one small study (n=20) and has not been replicated. No clinical trials have linked creatine supplementation to measurable hair loss outcomes — the jump from elevated DHT to follicle miniaturisation remains speculative based on current evidence.
